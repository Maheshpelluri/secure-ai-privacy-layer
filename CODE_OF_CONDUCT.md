# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This project is a research and architectural effort, and it depends on disagreement being possible without it becoming personal. Participants are expected to:

- Engage with ideas critically and directly, while treating the people behind them respectfully.
- Assume good faith, particularly when a proposal is early-stage or incomplete — this project is deliberately in that state.
- Back architectural or technical disagreement with reasoning, not assertion.
- Welcome contributors regardless of experience level, background, or which part of the stack (research, architecture, writing, code) they contribute to.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language or jokes, sustained disruption of discussion, and publishing others' private information without consent.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces — Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and any other official communication channel — and when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers via the repository's Issues (marked confidential where the platform supports it) or through the contact method listed in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md). Maintainers will review and respond to all reports, and are obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other project leadership.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted in spirit from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.1, simplified for an early-stage research repository.
